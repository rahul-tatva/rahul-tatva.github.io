Former Strictly Come Dancing star Kevin Clifton packed on the PDA with girlfriend Stacey Dooley as they attended the In The Heights screening at Back Yard Cinema in Wandsworth, London on Wednesday.

The dancer, 38, wrapped his arm around his presenter love, 34, as they enjoyed the film premiere, with the couple risking an awkward run-in with Kevin's ex-wife Karen Hauer, 39, who was also at the bash. 

The couple appeared in great spirits as they chatted and laughed outside the south London venue, with Kevin appearing to carry a snack in his hands. 

This Is My House host Stacey sizzled in a black, one-shouldered top, which she teamed with edgy jeans and black heels.

She rounded off her look with black sunglasses and left her auburn tresses loose.

Venezuelan beauty Karen, who split from Kevin in 2018 after three years of marriage, appeared in great spirits as she flashed a dazzling smile while showing off her toned physique in the skimpy top.

Karen rocked her signature cropped blonde locks, while highlighting her natural beauty with a light make-up palette.

Earlier this year, Karen said she would 'never, never have let go of her marriage' to Kevin and wanted to fight to keep it going. 

Karen told The Divorce Club podcast with Samantha Baines that the couple split after 'things got really nasty overnight' after becoming jealous at not getting enough attention from her partner. 

She said: 'Obviously, other things happen, there are other factors that happened, that really hurt us both. But I thought... I would have never let go of it. I wouldn’t have never, never let go of it. 

'I would have liked to have fought for it. It took me a year after we separated to actually let go of him.’ 

During the chat, Karen admitted that it took her a while to get over her relationship with Kevin, which is no surprise given that they had to work together on Strictly.

She continued: 'It took me a long time to cut the umbilical cord that I had connected to him. 

'Because it was connected for such a long time and at a completely different level than my first marriage, because this marriage was what I was always looking for. But I learned a lot.

'Huge lessons for me. Because of that breakup and what I had to go through emotionally, physically work wise and in the public eye - it made me stronger, and it made me realise what I really need to work on, and that was me.

‘I had to work with him for two, three years after we got separated and it was hard because you can see how much love you have for one another, and the respect.' 

Elsewhere, Tallia Storm was sure to turn heads as she flashed her taut midriff in a white crop top, which she teamed with a dazzling pink, purple and white patterned skirt, pink heels and a green handbag.

The trio were in good company at the event as Strictly's head judge Shirley Ballas was also in attendance.  

The dancer looked fabulous in a sharp white blazer and showstopping red heels as she posed for snaps with her beloved mother Audrey.

The ladies were also accompanied by Shirley's dapper boyfriend Daniel Taylor. 

Love Island's Amy Hart put on a demure display in a patterned midi dress which she teamed with taupe sandals. 

While radio presenter Sian Welby put on a very leggy display in a pattered red and white co-ord consisting of a blazer and thigh-skimming shorts. 

Meanwhile, Dancing On Ice's Vanessa Bauer turned up the heat as she slipped into a racy leopard print dress for the evening.

The ice skater looked stunning as she teamed the skimpy ensemble with black killer heels, while she wore her brunette tresses in loose waves. 

Elsewhere, radio presenter Daisy Maskell opted for a somewhat more low-key look, donning a mint broderie anglaise top teamed with flared, ripped jeans and white trainers. 

In The Heights is a film adaptation of Lin-Manuel Miranda's Broadway show, which is set to be released in UK cinemas on 18 June. 

Advertisement

Warner Bros. described the motion picture adaptation in a synopsis, 'The scent of a cafecito caliente hangs in the air just outside of the 181st Street subway stop. 

'The likeable, magnetic bodega owner Usnavi saves every penny from his daily grind as he hopes, imagines and sings about a better life.' 

Crazy Rich Asians filmmaker Jon M. Chu is directing the film, with Hamilton's Quiara Alegría Hudes having penned the script.

Advertisement

The musical was a huge hit on Broadway, having garnered four Tony Awards in 2008 for Best Musical, Best Original Score, and Best Choreography; and a Best Musical Show Album Grammy.
